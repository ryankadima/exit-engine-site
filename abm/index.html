The problem

Most "ABM" is theater.

A spreadsheet of 2,000 logos isn't a target account list. It's an unfiltered TAM with a fancier name. Ads running against that list aren't ABM either. They're awareness spend with no ground game behind them. Here's what actually breaks ABM programs, in order:

01

No coverage.

One contact per account, and that contact is an individual contributor. You're running campaigns at companies that need six signers, and reaching the one person who can't sign anything.

02

No tiers.

Every account gets the same touch, the same budget, the same intensity. Best-fit accounts get under-served, worst-fit accounts get over-served, and the whole program reads as flat and unremarkable.

03

No alignment.

Marketing runs plays against accounts sales has already written off. Sales has its own list, in its own head, that nobody else can see. The feedback loop dies before it starts.

04

No system.

It worked because one person was good at their job. The day they leave, or get pulled onto something else, it stops working.

That last one is the point

An ABM program that depends on a hero isn't a program. It's a habit one person has, with a quota attached.

Things people ask first.

We already have a target account list. Isn't that ABM?+
A list is a spreadsheet. ABM is what happens when that list is scored, watched for live signals, covered by mapped contacts, and worked by sales and marketing off the same definitions. Most companies have the spreadsheet. Almost none have the system.
We're too small for ABM. Isn't this an enterprise thing?+
No. The principle scales down to 25–50 accounts and a founder-led sales motion just as well as it scales up to a 1,000-account enterprise program. What changes is the personalization budget, not the discipline.
How is this different from just buying 6sense or Demandbase?+
A platform executes a motion you've already designed. Most companies buy the platform first and design the motion never. That's how you end up with an expensive tool and no actual program. We design the motion, prove it manually, then decide what you actually need to buy.
