7 Core Workflows — run in order · WF4–7 run weekly · WF1–3 quarterly/monthly
Workflow 01 Quarterly
Vertical Intelligence Engine

Pulls 90 days of closed-won from CRM, runs Claude pattern analysis to identify winning verticals, and generates structured TAL rules for lookalike discovery. The foundation everything else builds on.

Workflow 02 Per Interview
Proof Asset Factory

Processes 9-minute buyer interview recordings into case studies, quote banks, testimonial blocks, ad snippets, and sales proof cards. All proof is real — no fabricated social proof.

Workflow 03 Monthly
Lookalike Account + Contact Discovery

DiscoLike + Ocean.io find account lookalikes seeded from closed-won. AI Ark finds people lookalikes matching champion and buyer profiles. CRM deduplication removes existing customers.

Workflow 04 Weekly
Signal Matching + Account Tiering

Pulls hiring signals, tech installs, and CRM engagement. Claude scores each account and assigns Tier 1 (signal + engagement within 28 days), Tier 2, or Tier 3. Only Tier 1 gets direct outbound.

Workflow 05 Weekly
Pain Signal → Problem Statement Engine

Transforms raw signals into one-line problem statements per contact. The transformation chain: observed signal → inferred pain → business consequence → problem statement → supporting proof.

Workflow 06 Weekly
Messaging Engine

Generates outbound-ready copy per contact and channel using the 4-beat narrative: proof opener → problem statement → pain signal echo → evidence + CTA. Adapts tone, length, and format per channel.

Workflow 07 Weekly
Multi-Channel Distribution

Routes approved messaging to Outreach sequences (Tier 1), LinkedIn ABM ads via n8n (Tier 1+2), and LinkedIn DMs. All actions write back to CRM and Notion with full pipeline attribution.

WF1 — Vertical Intelligence Analysis 
System: You are a GTM strategist analyzing closed-won CRM data to identify the highest-converting market segments.

Analyze the following closed-won deals and return:
1. Top 3-5 verticals ranked by: win rate, average ACV, and average close time
2. For each vertical: 3-5 firmographic attributes most common in winning accounts
3. For each vertical: the job titles that appear most often as primary contacts
4. Patterns that cross verticals (shared attributes of all winning deals)
5. Any verticals with <5 deals — flag as 'insufficient sample'
6. A confidence score (0-100) for each vertical pattern based on sample size

Disqualify any pattern that appears in fewer than 30% of deals in that vertical.

WF2 — Proof Asset Extraction 
System: You are a B2B case study editor. Extract structured proof elements from the following customer interview transcript.

Extract and return:
1. Purchase reason — the primary problem that drove the purchase decision
2. Before state — specific description of what was broken or painful before purchase
3. After state — specific description of what improved after purchase
4. Measurable outcome — any specific number, percentage, or time metric mentioned
5. Emotional language — phrases the customer used that sound human and visceral (not corporate)
6. Proof snippets — 3-5 standalone quote fragments usable in ads, emails, or DMs
7. Testimonial — a 2-3 sentence polished testimonial the customer would approve
8. Confidence score — your confidence in the accuracy of each extraction (0-100)

Transcript: {{$json.interview_transcript}}
Customer: {{$json.customer_name}}, {{$json.customer_title}}, {{$json.vertical}}

Return as structured JSON with confidence score per field.

WF5 — Signal to Problem Statement 
System: You are a B2B messaging strategist. Transform raw signals into problem statements that describe a buyer's current reality in their own language.

Account: {{$json.account_name}}
Contact: {{$json.contact_name}}, {{$json.contact_title}}
Vertical: {{$json.vertical}}
Detected pain signals: {{$json.signals_detected}}
Primary pain hypothesis: {{$json.primary_pain_hypothesis}}
Purchase reasons from similar won accounts: {{$json.purchase_reasons}}

Transformation framework:
1. Observed signal → what is actually happening at this company
2. Inferred pain → what business problem this signal reveals
3. Likely business consequence → what the pain is costing them
4. Problem statement → one sentence in buyer language that captures the pain
5. Supporting proof → which case study or quote best matches this pain
6. Best CTA angle → what offer is most relevant given this pain

Adapt the problem statement to sound right to a {{$json.contact_title}}, not a generic "decision maker."

Return as structured JSON with confidence score.
WF6 — Email Message Generation 
System: You are a B2B outbound copywriter. Write a cold email using the four-beat narrative exactly.

Inputs:
- Similar company: {{$json.proof_company}}
- Outcome achieved: {{$json.proof_outcome}}
- Problem statement: {{$json.problem_statement}}
- Pain signal observed: {{$json.pain_signal_evidence}}
- Case study link: {{$json.case_study_url}}
- Recipient: {{$json.contact_name}}, {{$json.contact_title}}
- Vertical: {{$json.vertical}}

Rules:
1. Subject line: 6 words or fewer. No clickbait.
2. Email body: 5-7 sentences total.
3. Do not mention your company name in the first two sentences.
4. Do not use: leverage, optimize, synergy, world-class.
5. Pain signal echo must reference something observable — not "I noticed you visited our site."
6. CTA: One specific, low-commitment ask.

Return JSON: { subject_line, email_body, confidence, personalization_elements_used }

Getting Started

Implementation: Where to Start

You don't need to build all 7 workflows at once. Here's the order that creates value fastest:

Week 1–2: Data layer + Vertical Intelligence (WF1)

Before anything runs, your CRM data has to be clean. The vertical scorecard is the foundation every downstream workflow depends on. Getting this right in week one means you don't rebuild it in month two.

Week 2: Lookalike Discovery + Signal Matching (WF3 + WF4)

With TAL rules from WF1, start building the account list immediately. Run DiscoLike and Ocean.io while customer interviews are being scheduled. By end of Week 2 you should have a Tier 1 list ready for messaging.

Week 2–3: Proof Assets (WF2)

Interview scheduling is the long-lead item. Start sourcing in Week 1, run interviews in Week 2. You can run a lightweight first messaging cycle with a quote bank even before the full case study is approved.

Week 3–4: Messaging + Distribution (WF5 + WF6 + WF7)

With a Tier 1 list, proof assets, and problem statements ready, the messaging and distribution workflows run in a single week. First Tier 1 sequences should be live by Day 20.

The 30-day milestone: By Day 30, you should have your first 20–50 Tier 1 contacts enrolled in a sequence, LinkedIn ABM ads live for Tier 2, your first case study published, and your first reply data in. The weekly operating cadence takes over from there.
